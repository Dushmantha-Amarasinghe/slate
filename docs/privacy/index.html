Privacy Policy

Last updated July 3, 2026
Short version: Slate is an offline-first app. There's no account, no server, no analytics, and no ad tracking. Your tasks, tags, and voice notes are stored only on your device, inside the app's own private storage.

What Slate stores

Everything you create in Slate — tasks, tags, subtasks, reminders, and settings — is saved in a local database on your device. Voice notes you record are saved as audio files in the app's private storage folder. None of this is uploaded anywhere; Slate has no backend server to send it to.

Permissions Slate asks for, and why

  • Notifications — to show you task reminders at the time you set.
  • Exact alarms — so reminders fire at the precise time you chose, not "sometime around" it.
  • Microphone — only used when you tap the record button to attach a voice note to a task. Nothing is recorded in the background.
  • Battery optimization exemption — optional, and only requested if you enable it in Settings. Some phone manufacturers aggressively kill background apps, which can delay or drop reminders; this permission asks Android not to do that to Slate.
  • Run at startup — used to reschedule your reminders after your phone reboots, since scheduled alarms don't survive a restart on Android.
  • Install unknown apps — Slate isn't distributed through the Play Store, so this is required by Android before it will let you install an updated APK downloaded through the app's own "Check for updates" feature.
  • Internet — used only to check GitHub for a newer app version and to download the update file if you choose to install it. No other data is sent or received.

Data export and backup

Settings → Data Management lets you export your tasks to a JSON file, or import one back in. That file is written to storage you choose and is never transmitted anywhere by Slate — it's yours to keep, move, or delete.

What Slate doesn't do

  • No account or sign-in of any kind.
  • No analytics, crash reporting, or usage tracking.
  • No ads, and no third-party ad or tracking SDKs.
  • No data is sold, shared, or transmitted to anyone — Slate simply has no server to send it to.

Deleting your data

Uninstalling Slate removes its local database and any recorded voice notes along with it. You can also clear individual tasks, clear all completed tasks, or wipe everything from Settings → Data Management without uninstalling.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the "Last updated" date above will change too, and the new version will be published at this same address.
